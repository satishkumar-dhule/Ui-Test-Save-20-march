# Technical Leadership at Google

## What Makes a Good Tech Lead

### Core Skills

1. **Technical Breadth**
   - Understand enough to make decisions across areas
   - Know when to bring in specialists
   - Stay technically relevant

2. **Delegation**
   - Trust your team
   - Let people make mistakes and learn
   - Don't micromanage

3. **Communication**
   - Translate technical to business
   - Translate business to technical
   - Write clearly

4. **Mentorship**
   - Grow skills of those around you
   - Provide feedback
   - Career development

5. **Decision Making**
   - Make timely decisions
   - Work with incomplete information
   - Own your decisions

## Delegation Techniques

### The Delegation Ladder

1. **Tell**: Do exactly what I say
2. **Sell**: Explain why this is important
3. **Consult**: Ask for input, then decide
4. **Agree**: Mutual decision
5. **Advise**: Guide their decision
6. **Inquire**: Ask what they decided
7. **Delegate**: Full authority

### When to Use Each Level

- **Tell**: Emergencies, safety issues
- **Sell**: Critical buy-in needed
- **Consult**: Need diverse input
- **Agree**: Strategic decisions
- **Advise**: Growing team members
- **Inquire**: Trusted senior members
- **Delegate**: Fully autonomous

## Running Effective Meetings

### Before the Meeting

- Have a clear agenda
- Send agenda in advance
- Limit attendees to necessary people

### During the Meeting

- Start on time
- Keep focused on agenda
- Encourage participation
- Make decisions when possible

### After the Meeting

- Send notes within 24 hours
- Include action items and owners
- Follow up on commitments

## One-on-Ones

### Purpose

- Build trust and rapport
- Discuss career growth
- Address challenges
- Give feedback

### Structure

1. **Open** (5 min): How are you?
2. **Updates** (10 min): What happened since last time?
3. **Deep Dive** (20 min): Discuss topic of their choice
4. **Career** (10 min): Growth and goals
5. **Feedback** (5 min): How can I help?

### Tips

- Meet regularly (weekly/bi-weekly)
- Use the same time slot
- Don't cancel unless essential
- Focus on them, not status

## Technical Decision Making

### The RAPID Framework

- **R**ecommend: Make a proposal
- **A**gree: Must sign off
- **P**erform: Execute the decision
- **I**nput: Provide input
- **D**ecide: Make final call

### Decision Rights

1. **Team Lead**: Technical direction
2. **Tech Lead**: Cross-team technical
3. **Product**: Features and priorities
4. **Engineering Manager**: People and process

## Managing Up

### Understanding Your Manager

- What are their priorities?
- How do they prefer communication?
- What information do they need?

### Tips

- Keep them informed
- Don't surprise them
- Ask for help early
- Understand their constraints

## Handling Conflict

### The Thomas-Kilmann Model

1. **Competing**: Win-lose
2. **Collaborating**: Win-win
3. **Compromising**: Lose some, win some
4. **Avoiding**: Lose-lose
5. **Accommodating**: Win-lose (other wins)

### Conflict Resolution Steps

1. Understand both perspectives
2. Find common ground
3. Focus on facts, not feelings
4. Look for creative solutions
5. Agree on next steps

## Building High-Performing Teams

### Team Norms

- Code review expectations
- Meeting schedules
- Communication channels
- Decision making process

### Psychological Safety

- It's OK to make mistakes
- Questions are valued
- Diverse perspectives welcome
- Criticism is constructive

### Technical Excellence

- High standards for code
- Regular tech talks
- Continuous learning
- Knowledge sharing

## Career Development

### The Technical Ladder

1. **IC Track**: Individual Contributor
   - Junior → Mid → Senior → Staff → Principal

2. **Management Track**
   - Tech Lead → Manager → Senior Manager → Director

### Skills at Each Level

| Level     | Focus                       |
| --------- | --------------------------- |
| Junior    | Learning, executing         |
| Mid       | Independent, mentoring      |
| Senior    | Leading technical work      |
| Staff     | Cross-team impact           |
| Principal | Organization-wide influence |

## Key Takeaways

1. Delegate more than you think you should
2. Meet regularly with your team
3. Make decisions and own them
4. Communicate clearly to multiple audiences
5. Focus on team growth
6. Build psychological safety
7. Keep learning and growing
